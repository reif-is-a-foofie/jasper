# Jasper Product Plan

## Vision snapshot

- Jasper makes connectors, the calendar tool, and email aware only after clear approval. Every connector must pass a gating step before it shows up in the terminal so the operator keeps control.
- Jasper keeps these systems simple and brave: once the connector is on, the calendar tool can answer “what changed on my schedule” and the email lanes can summarize the inbox without ever sharing secrets without consent.
- The plan is to keep running Jasper in terminal mode, let it monitor itself, and let the watchdog loop keep fixing what breaks.
- We are building a co-founder for life, so nothing is complete until the five brain regions are strong enough to self-heal, upgrade, and speak up when judgment is needed.

## Five regions distilled

1. **Self Model** – identity, onboarding, and health checks that make Jasper aware of who it is and what mission it serves.
2. **Perception & Attention** – connectors, calendar tool reads, email gating, and monitoring that let Jasper feel the household heartbeat.
3. **Memory & World Model** – short-term working memory, reflections, and long-term commitments that remember what happened and who matters.
4. **Executive & Action** – planning, workflows, tool calls, and delegation that turn intention into results; caretaker of the calendar tool and downstream actions.
5. **Regulation & Growth** – trust, approvals, secret stewardship, self-evaluation, and upgrade loops that keep Jasper safe and improving.

## Milestones 1 through 10

- Milestones 1–2 gave us the identity, launch path, and memory stack we needed.
- Milestone 3 added the tool scaffold so Jasper could call things like `jasper/calendar`.
- Milestone 4 started the listeners, and Milestone 5 built the dream-state reflections.
- Milestones 6–8 delivered tool generation, brokerage, onboarding, and authentication so a new operator can set up Jasper without asking for help.
- Milestone 9 is running now: connectors, activation/approval flows, calendar and email tools, and the gating flows that keep the data safe.
- Milestone 10 is next: keep the active connectors, run background monitors, and deliver the daily digest so Jasper is proactively useful even before the operator types a new prompt.

## Next move

- Continue straight into Milestone 10. Keep the terminal experience meaningful by testing the calendar tool flows, watching connectors' gating, and letting Jasper run on its own while you sleep.
- Keep note of every decision so we can continue unattended development with clear judgment records and self-correction signals.
